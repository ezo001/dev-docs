DOCX-to-web conversion job aid

Job aid / procedural doc · Case study · Sanitized for public portfolio

Problem

Subject matter experts were comfortable in Word but the publishing target was a Git-backed Markdown site. Without a clear job aid, authors either avoided the new pipeline or introduced formatting debt (broken tables, EMF images, manual sidebar edits).

My role

I wrote a step-by-step job aid covering download, conversion, local preview, fix-up checklist, commit, and deploy — plus a companion "Word do's and don'ts" and MDX compatibility checklist.

Job aid structure

  1. Prerequisites — tools installed, folder layout, which asset folder to use
  2. Step 1: Download — get the approved DOCX from the right library
  3. Step 2: Convert — run the conversion script; where output lands
  4. Step 3: Review locally — npm start, what to check (images, headings, links)
  5. Step 4: Fix — MDX checklist (admonitions, imports, broken media)
  6. Step 5: Publish — branch, PR, merge, verify on staging URL
  7. Troubleshooting — top 5 conversion failures with fixes

Writing principles applied

  • One action per step — no compound instructions
  • Verbs lead steps — "Download", "Run", "Open", "Fix", "Push"
  • Screenshots only where UI is ambiguous — text-first for maintainability
  • Escape hatch — when to escalate to Doc Team vs. self-serve

Writing excerpt (sanitized)

## Step 3: Review locally

1. From the repo root, run: npm start
2. Open http://localhost:3000 and navigate to your doc.
3. Check:
   - Title and sidebar label look correct
   - Images render (not broken icons)
   - Tables are readable on narrow viewports
   - Internal links resolve

If something looks wrong, fix the .md file — do not re-export from Word
unless the source content changed.

Outcomes

  • Primary onboarding doc for authors moving off SharePoint-only workflows
  • Reduced "how do I publish?" questions in team channels
  • Paired with video walkthrough for visual learners (optional supplement)
